Understanding Blood Types

There are four main blood groups: A, B, AB, and O. Each blood group can be either Rh-positive (+) or Rh-negative (-).

Group A

Has A antigens on the red cells and B antibodies in the plasma.

Group B

Has B antigens on the red cells and A antibodies in the plasma.

Group AB

Has both A and B antigens on red cells and no antibodies in the plasma. It's the universal plasma donor.

Group O

Has no antigens on red cells but both A and B antibodies in the plasma. It's the universal red cell donor.

Rh Factor

The presence (+) or absence (-) of the Rh antigen also matters. Rh-negative blood can be given to Rh-positive patients, but not vice versa.
